1 - pharmacodynamics

Pharmacology is study of substances that interact with living systems through chemical processes, especially by binding to regulatory molecules and activating or inhibiting normal body processes. Effects can be therapeutic (medical pharmacology) or toxic (toxicology). A drug is a substance that brings about change in the body and can be synthesized by the body or foreign. Toxins are poisons of biological origins. Pharmacodynamics action of drugs on the body. Pharmacokinetics are actions of the body on the drug - ADME (absorption, distribution, metaoblism, excretion).

  • principal - the use of the drug (it have some intensity) or secondary (beneficial or adverse) - small intensity
  • local (topical): skin, mucosa, gut or general (systemic)
  • reversibile - that last till the inactivation/elimination of the substance or irreversible
  • can be receptor mediated or non-receptor mediated

3 - parasympathomimetics

A receptor is cholinergic if it uses Acetylcholine, which is a biogenic amine synthetized in the body from choline and acetylcoenzime. A under the action colinacetil-transferase. Ach released from presynaptic endings can bind to cholinergic receptors (activating them) or acetylcholinersterase (inactivating Ach). The cholinesterase system includes; Acetylcholinesterase is present in cholingergic neruons, cholinergic synapses and other tissues and pseudocholinesterase in the plasma, liver, intestines, skin, glial cells and other tissues. The result is choline (can be reused in Ach syntehsis) and acetic acid (in krebs cycle). Within 2-3msec the released Ach interacts with the receptor on the postsynaptic membrane causing stimulation of the nerve.Parasympathomimetics (cholinergics, cholinergic agonists) produce similar effects of parasympathetic stimulation and activation of muscarinic and nicotinic neuroeffector cholinergic synapses.

  • b) Irreversible: Ecothiopate, Metrifonate, Fluostigmine, Paraoxon, Sarin


Nicotinic receptors are present on the ganglia, while muscarinic receptors are present on the target tissue. Muscarinic receptors are specifically activated by muscarine (Amanita muscaria), and there are 5 subtypes: neuroeffector parasympathetic synapses in smooth muscle, heart, and exocrine glands. Neuroeffector sympathetic synapses in sweat glands and brain. Nicotinic receptors are specifically activated by nicotine (Tobacco). 2 subtypes. Nm (N1) in neuromuscular junction. Nn (N2) in autonomic interneuronal and adrenal medula (medulosuprarenal gland).
